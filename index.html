h1>Walk to Hadimba temple

The epic Mahabharata narrates that the Pandavas had stayed in Himachal during their exile, and during that time, the strongest of the Pandavas, Bhima, married the demoness Hadimba.
After a few years, when Bhima and his brothers were to return home from exile, Hadimba did not accompany them. Instead, she stayed back and did tapasya, a combination of meditation, prayer, and penance, so as to eventually attain the status of a goddess

The Hadimba temple is an ancient structure built as an homage to Hadmba devi, who is said to have lived and meditated in the area. The temple is less than 10 minutes away from the property by foot.

Explore the outdoors

There are a few short and easy, yet beautiful walks from the house which guests may take.

One can take the trail that leads to old Manali which will take you through the beautiful forest with its many birds and flowers. This walk is made even more beautiful in the time of spring, when all the surrounding fields of apples and cherries are covered with white and pink blossoms.

There is another short-cut to old Manali while walking, which gets you there in 10-15 minutes, which is especially helpful when you wish to avoid traffic. Similarly, one can also walk to the Manali town through the Hadimba temple area, which also takes roughly the same amount of time.

There is another beautiful area nearby, which is the wildlife sanctuary. This sanctuary is an enclosed part of the forest and has many different paths to walk and explore. It has its own charm in the summers, when it is significantly cooler than the rest of town and has unparalleled beauty in the winters, where the lake in the center gets frozen solid!

Visit cafes in Old Manali and the Town

Manali is famous for its cafes and restaurants. It has had a huge influx of international and domestic travelers and settlers over the years, and that has resulted in a melting pot of different cultures and traditions.

No matter what cuisine one craves for, there will definitely be a place serving authentic food for it here, and a 20 minute walk at maximum from the property or a 10 minute drive will get you to most of these places.

Trek to Lama Dug

A four hour uphill walk from the house leads to a vast beautiful meadow know as lama dug(3,170 mtr). From there, one can get stunning panoramic views of Deo Tibba, Hanuman Tibba and many great peaks. It is a fairly easy hike to complete, and yet offers some of the most rewarding views from anywhere in the valley. The only things required for this day trip are a good pair of hiking shoes and some packed lunch.
